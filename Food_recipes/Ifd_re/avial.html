avial

Avial is an Indian dish with origins in the Kerala region. Its name means to be cooked, and the dish consists of a combination of vegetables, coconut, and curry leaves.It is an essential dish of Sadya (a festive vegetarian feast served on plantain leaf).

Avial also called as aviyal is a popular and traditional mix vegetable dish from the Kerala cuisine, though you can also find this dish prepared in parts of Tamil Nadu and Karnataka.Serve with steamed rice, sambar, pickle and some papadoms for a hearty and satisfying meal.

ingredients
  • 1 cup chopped ash gourd (white pumpkin, winter melon, petha)
  • 1 cup chopped pumpkin
  • 1 cup chopped drumsticks or 1 to 2 drumsticks (moringa pods, sahjan ki phalli)
  • 1 cup chopped elephant foot yam (suran)
  • 1 cup chopped mangalore cucumber (field marrow, madras cucumber)
  • 1 cup chopped plantain (raw unripe banana)
  • ½ cup chopped french beans (green beans)
  • 1 cup fresh grated coconut
  • 2 to 3 green chilies
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • ⅓ to ½ cup water, for grinding
  • 1 cup water, for cooking veggies
  • 1 cup Curd (yogurt), beaten
  • 12 to 15 curry leaves
  • 1 to 2 tablespoons coconut oil
