kheer

Kheer or payasam is an ancient Indian sweets, a creamy rice pudding that is made in several versions across the country.Kheer is like a pudding traditionally made with milk, sugar and some dry fruits.The choice of spices and flavorings are usually cardamom, rose water, kewra water or saffron.

Rice kheer is basically Indian version of rice pudding made with basmati rice, whole milk, sugar, nuts and spices.In South Indian cuisine kheer is called as payasam. It is believed that the sweet originated 2000 years ago in the Lord Jagannath Temple in Orissa.

ingredients
  • ¼ cup basmati rice
  • 1 litre whole milk – 4 cups
  • 6 tablespoons sugar or add as required
  • ½ teaspoon cardamom powder or 5 to 6 green cardamoms powdered in mortar-pestle(choti elaichi powder)
  • 1 pinch saffron strands or 14 to 16 saffron strands
  • 1 tablespoon chopped or sliced almonds or blanched almonds
  • 1 tablespoon chopped or sliced cashews
  • 1 tablespoon chopped or sliced unsalted pistachios
  • 1 tablespoon golden raisins
